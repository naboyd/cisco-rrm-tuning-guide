Classic WLC vs Catalyst Center AI-Enhanced RRM — Control Matrix

Use this matrix to decide where to make a change and whether it will take effect. Cisco splits RRM into a cloud Resource Analytics Engine (DCA/TPC/FRA/DBS) and a local Edge Compute Engine (CHD, ED-RRM, DFS, roaming).

How to tell which mode you are in (run on WLC) show ap dot11 5ghz group — Classic: Group Role: Auto-Leader, leader = your WLC hostname/IP. AI: Group Role: Remote-Member, leader = Catalyst Center (often shows as a DNA Center / CatC RRM leader name). Also: show wireless rf-group summary and Catalyst Center → Assurance → AI RRM Control Center (if enrolled).
Rules of thumb (1) One RRM algorithm per WLC — all sites on that controller share classic or AI, not both. (2) If an AI RF Profile service (DCA/TPC/FRA/DBS) is enabled, tune it in Catalyst Center → AI RF Profile; WLC global RRM CLI for that service is effectively overridden. (3) Edge settings (CHD, ED-RRM) stay on the WLC even in AI mode — but Insights may recommend changes in CatC. (4) WLAN/client settings (band select, steering) are always WLC, never AI RRM. (5) Match AI RF Profile schema to WLC RF profile (e.g. DBS max width) or APs may not get the profile on failover.

Decision flow

  1. Run show ap dot11 5ghz group → Auto-Leader on WLC = classic; Remote-Member = AI enrolled.
  2. Is the change DCA, TPC, FRA, or DBS? → If AI enrolled, use AI RF Profile (enable that service + busy hour/sensitivity). WLC ap dot11 … rrm channel/tpc changes for that service will not drive behavior.
  3. Is the change CHD, ED-RRM, CleanAir event, or DFS? → WLC (edge engine), even in AI mode. Check CatC Insights for recommendations only.
  4. Is the change band select, client steering, 802.11k/v, SSID, flex? → WLC / WLAN always.
  5. Is the change RxSOP, data rates, client-network-preference, tx-power min/max caps? → RF Profile: WLC RF profile (classic) or AI RF Profile push (AI). Keep schemas aligned.
  6. After AI profile push, confirm show ap tag summary shows AI-assigned RF tag and show ap dot11 5ghz summary reflects expected width/power.

Catalyst 9800 — Classic RRM

Local Auto-Leader. Configuration → Radio Configurations → RRM.

Client Preference & FRA client guardrails Client Network Preference (client-network-preference) is set per RF profile — values: default, 5ghz, 6ghz. It biases capable clients toward a band; it is not the same as WLAN band select. Client-aware FRA uses client-select (% of clients already on 5 GHz before converting a 2.4 GHz radio) and client-reset (minimum associated clients before an FRA band-change decision resets). These are the primary "number of clients before radio change" knobs for FRA — separate from CHD min failed clients (power) and client steering client-count (roam).

Catalyst Center 3.x — AI-Enhanced RRM

Prerequisites Enable AI Network Analytics and Device Controllability. Assign AI RF Profile per site. Insights populate after ~1 week. Profile push causes CAPWAP reset — schedule maintenance window.
